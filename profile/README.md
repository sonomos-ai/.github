## The Problem

AI has become the default interface for work, research, and communication. Every day, millions of people disclose sensitive information — names, addresses, financial records, medical details — into AI that they don't control.

Most of this exposure is unintentional. The tooling hasn't caught up to the threat model.

**There is no undo button once data reaches a third-party model.** Training pipelines ingest it. Logs retain it. Breach surfaces multiply. Privacy policies disclaim responsibility. And the person who typed it rarely knew what they shared.

## What Sonomos Does About It

We build tools that sit between you and AI — detecting sensitive data before it leaves your device and giving you the choice to mask, redact, or proceed.

No cloud processing. No server-side scanning. **Your data never has to leave your machine to be protected.**

We treat every AI interface as an exfiltration surface and every keystroke as a privacy decision that deserves tooling — not just policy.

## What We Believe

**Privacy is a design problem, not a policy problem.** Consent banners and privacy policies shift responsibility to the user. We think the tooling itself should make exposure difficult by default.

**Local-first is non-negotiable.** Detection, analysis, and protection happen on your machine. We don't need to see your data to protect it, and we've built Sonomos to prove it.

**AI changes the threat model.** The interface between humans and AI systems is where privacy breaks down — and where it needs to be enforced.

## What We're Building

We develop our privacy infrastructure to work across multiple surfaces — browsers, desktops, and the workflows between them. Our tools detect and intervene on sensitive data exposure in real time, using on-device intelligence that requires zero cloud dependency for core functionality.

The specifics evolve. The principle doesn't: **catch it before it leaves.**

## Our Stack

To ensure protection for all surfacees, we work with a range of technologies depending on the surface.

We favor local inference, browser-native APIs, and cryptographic primitives over server-side processing wherever possible.
